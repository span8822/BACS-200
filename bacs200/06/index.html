Dangers of Technology

An overuse of technology can lead to mental health issues in teens and adults alike
An overuse of technology-specifiacally social media-can lead to battling depression and anxiety
Social media can also lead to self esteem issues
An increase in instant gratification and need for approval of others can lead to anxiety
All of these factors are influenced by unrealistic body standards in front of users faces daily,
a need for constant approval, and depression when these expecations are not lived up to in their own minds.

An over use of technology can also lead to neglect in every day life.
Using technology too much means it's getting all of your attention, none of it is going towards your real life.
Real life involves a social life, chores, and work. When you neglect your social life, you are harming your social skills.
Due to a lack of face-to-face interaction, you are less able to read social cues and tonal cues and are more likely
to upset yourself or another person. This can lead to a depression from lack of proper socialization.
When you allow your space to fall to disarray, this can cause your anxiety and stress to spike.
You'll be so focused on the fact that you need to clean or do some sort of work that you might end up
putting off all forms of work in order to relax on the device that's causing you to let your space get messy.

Too much technology can also cause sleep issues.
The blue light emitted from device screens has been known to keep users awake.
There are filters and technology enhancements to keep the blue light to a minimum, but the mental
stimulation that comes from actively using something can also keep you from falling asleep.
With a lack of sleep. the brain can start hallucinating and can cause irritability and depression.
Also humans can die if they don't get enough sleep. Moral of the story is, you are far more important than your device.
Take care of yourself.
